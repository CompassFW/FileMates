# FileMates — Vision

> **Get your Friday evenings back.**

## Why

Exhausted from cleaning up after your inbox — and it still takes forever to find anything?

Hunting down receipts, downloading attachments, renaming them, sorting them away — evening
after evening. For anyone short on time, focus or patience, it quietly piles up: everything
lands unsorted in one folder, nothing is findable, and the year-end handover to the tax advisor
turns expensive and frustrating.

**FileMates exists to take that whole load off your plate** — so you stop losing hours and
nerves to document chaos.

## What

A **macOS [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) skill** that **pulls every receipt,
invoice and document out of your email and files it on your disk** — named to your convention,
sorted into your structure, and **verified on disk**.

It can even **propose a naming scheme and label structure for you** — tailored to what it
finds in your inbox — the "what goes where, how to name it" know‑how you'd normally pay an
assistant or bookkeeper for, available to people who can't.

On top, it keeps your inbox tidy (labels, reminders for actionable mail, clearing clutter) — but
the heart is **download → file → done**.

**Who it's for:** self‑employed people & small companies — built first for the German
freelancer/UG reality (tax‑advisor handover, receipt‑keeping), but useful for anyone who wants
their email documents organized on disk. Works for everyone; designed first for those the pain
hits hardest.

## How

**Set it up once — with you, by your rules.** Then it runs: download → name → file → verify.

Safety is the foundation that makes it OK to let near your inbox:

- a **protected‑sender list** (tax advisor, bank, clients) that is **never** deleted;
- **never archive** a document mail until its file is **confirmed on disk**;
- **deletion only ever moves mail to Trash** (reversible); the assistant proposes deletions and acts only after you confirm — auto-trashing happens only for senders you explicitly listed, and only after the file is safely on disk;
- *(planned)* a periodic **deep safety re‑check** as a second backstop, so a document is never lost for good.

> Guiding principle: **never lose a real task, never delete protected mail, never archive a
> document until the file is safely on disk.**
